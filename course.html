EXPLORE MORE ABOUT TYPES OF TRIBES

Indigenous Tribes

These are ethnic groups that are native to a particular region or territory and have a deep connection to their ancestral lands. Indigenous tribes often possess unique cultural practices, spiritual beliefs, and traditional knowledge that have been passed down through generations.

Read More......

Nomadic Tribes

Nomadic tribes are groups of people who move from one place to another in search of food, water, and grazing grounds for their livestock. They do not have permanent settlements and are known for their adaptability to different environments.

Read More......


Pastoral Tribes

These tribes primarily rely on animal husbandry for their livelihoods. They raise and depend on domesticated animals such as cattle, sheep, goats, or camels for sustenance and trade.

Read More......


Hunter-Gathere Tribes

Hunter-gatherer tribes are among the oldest forms of human societies. They rely on hunting, fishing, and gathering wild plants and fruits for survival. These tribes are usually small in size and often semi-nomadic.

Read More......


Agrarian Tribes

Agrarian tribes are settled communities that practice agriculture as their primary means of subsistence. They cultivate crops and raise livestock in one location and generally have more stable living conditions compared to nomadic or hunter-gatherer tribes.

Read More......


Amazonian Tribes

Indigenous tribes residing in the Amazon rainforest of South America. These tribes have unique cultures and are often threatened by deforestation, modernization, and outside contact. They are considered highly endangered because of conflicts with logging interests in their territory. The Kawahiva live in the north of Mato Grosso.

North American Tribes

Native American tribes, such as the Navajo, Cherokee, Sioux, and Apache, among many others, have inhabited the North American continent for thousands of years.
